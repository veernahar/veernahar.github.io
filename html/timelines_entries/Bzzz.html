Bzzz

Background

In March 2020, at the start of the Covid-19 Pandemic, school had just been canceled, with plans to go remote in a week. My sister my mom and I were sitting on the couch talking, when my sister complained "when do we get to go back to school." That's when my mom had the idea to make a device that would buzz when you came within 6 feet of another person wearing the same device.

Bzzz v0

After researching for a while, I decided to prototype the idea first, using two raspberry pi's. The idea was the both devices would be transmitting and receiving bluetooth signals, and by comparing the signal strength of the received signals against a threshold, we would be able to determine roughly how far away the other devices were. Based on that, the devices would buzz/not buzz. I ordered the parts that I would need, and after a few weeks I had a prototype working. I made a python program to print out the signal strength of the other raspberry pi, and the results looked promising, so it was time to move on to v1.

Bzzz v1

Raspberry Pi's are very big, and for that reason I chose not to use them in v1. Instead, I opted for the Arduino compatible ESP32 dev board, which has a bluetooth chip on it. I soldered on a vibration motor, and got the same code from the Raspberry Pi prototype working in C++ on the EPS32's. After decided on a threshold that seemed to be somewhat close to 6 feet, and finding that it worked decently, I decided to contact my school to see if they had any interest in this project, since the whole point was to help with social distancing so that we could get back to school sooner.

Reaching out to School

I had a meeting with the school head and some other staff, and in my presentation I proposed a timeline and BOM that would end with the devices being ready before school started the next year and an initial proposed cost of 15 dollars per device. We ended the meeting, and they said they would talk it over and get back to us. Later that day, they emailed us saying they said they would sponsor the project by covering prototyping costs and buying 750 devices for a total budget of 10 thousand dollars.
