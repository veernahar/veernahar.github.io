Differential Turret

In the summer of 2021, I was learning how to use CAD software, so I decided to CAD and render a Differential Turret. A differential is a mechanism that has two inputs and an output which is the sum of the two inputs. Couple that with a turret, and the turret can now spin infinitely in either direction. This CAD challenge I set for myself taught me a lot about robot design as it was my first full robot I ever designed in CAD. It took over 30 hours to CAD and render.

Mecanum Gear Drivetrain

In FIRST Tech Challenge, people are always striving to make the most compact drivetrain possible. I set upon this challenge, and designed a drivetrain that uses gears that mesh with mecanum wheels to create drivepods that were just 40mm wide.

Belted Drivetrain

The Mecanum Gear Drivetrain I designed was very cool, but it wasnt practical. It cost a lot, and was more prone to failure. Because of this, I designed a drivetrain that would likely see more use in competitions.

Coaxial Swerve Module

A coaxial swerve module is a module with two inputs - one to rotate it, and one to rotate the wheel. If you put multiple of them on a robot, you acheive holonomic control without sacrificing traction.
